Giving Back

Community Chair-Massage Program

We bring on-site chair massage to local fundraisers and events, then split the proceeds 50/50, with the host's half going straight to a cause they choose. The fundraising is the value, no vendor booth fees attached.

The Idea

Relief at the event, money for the cause.

Seated chair massage is the easiest thing in the world to add to a gathering. Clothes stay on, no oil, no table, no downtime. People sit for a few minutes between everything else they came to do and walk away looser than they arrived.

The Elbow Room community program brings that setup to fundraisers, markets, gym events, and neighborhood get-togethers across Woodstock and the greater Atlanta metro. We split what comes in 50/50, and the host points their half at whatever cause they are raising for. We do not charge a booth fee or take a cut off the top. The event keeps the value it generates.

How It Works

Four steps, no fundraising headache

You handle the gathering. We handle the chairs. The split is simple enough to explain in one sentence.

1

The host reaches out

Tell us the date, the place, and the cause. A fundraiser, a market, a gym event, a block party: if people will be in one spot for a couple of hours, it works.

2

We bring the setup on-site

Chair, supplies, and a licensed therapist arrive ready to work. You point us at a corner with an outlet and we are running in minutes.

3

Attendees get quick relief

Short seated sessions, clothes on, no downtime. People pay for their few minutes in the chair the same way they would tip a good vendor.

4

Half the proceeds go to the cause

At the end we split what came in down the middle. Your half goes to the cause you named when you booked. No invoicing games, no fee skimmed off the top.

Pricing follows our standard seated rates and is preview pricing, subject to change. Chair sessions run $25 for 15 minutes or $45 for 30. We confirm the day's setup with you before the event.
Where This Works

Built for the gatherings you already throw

A few shapes the program tends to take. Yours does not have to look like any of these to fit.

A charity 5K or race

Runners cross the line and head straight for the chair to flush out the legs. The organizers send their half to the cause the race was run for.

A neighborhood block party

Set up next to the food and the music. Neighbors drop in between conversations, and the organizers send their half to a local fund the street already cares about.

A gym memorial workout

After the effort, recovery makes sense. Members cool down in the chair, and the gym directs its half toward the person or cause the workout honors.

Why The Split Works

Money spent here stays here.

A dollar that goes through a national chain leaves the neighborhood and does not come back. A dollar spent at your event stays in the loop: half pays a local therapist for honest work, half goes to a cause your community already chose.

The host wins

You add a real draw to your event and raise money without inventing a raffle or chasing sponsors. The chairs do the work.

The community wins

Attendees get a few minutes of actual relief, and the cause gets a real cut. Nobody is asked to donate out of guilt.

The incentives line up

We get paid for good work, you get a draw and a donation, the cause gets funded. No part of it depends on anyone feeling shaken down.

Good To Know

Hosting questions, answered

What does the 50/50 split actually mean?
Whatever the chairs bring in at your event gets divided in half. One half pays Elbow Room for the work. The other half goes to the cause you name when you book. There is no separate booth fee and nothing skimmed before the split.
Who picks the cause?
You do. The host directs their half to whatever they are raising for: a mutual aid fund, a family in need, a local nonprofit, a memorial. We do not gatekeep the cause.
What kind of events fit?
Fundraisers, markets, gym events, and community gatherings all work well. The main thing is a steady flow of people in one place for a couple of hours and a corner with an outlet for the chair.
What does it cost to host?
Nothing up front. There is no vendor fee to bring us in. Attendees pay our standard seated rates per session, and half of that goes to your cause. Rates are preview pricing and subject to change; we confirm the day's setup with you before the event.
